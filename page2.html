Global Refugee Populations & Development Assistance Allocations

Ever since the securitization of the migration flows, different institutions including states, IGOs, supranational organizations have adopted different measures. Often, these measures are harsh and exclusionary border practices in order to keep ‘third national countries’ excluded from potential host territories. Further, foreign aid has also become a tool for first world countries to direct their political agenda in order to strengthen these practices. To what degree is allocated assistance not need based but rather political ?This spatial analysis utilizes development assistance data for 2015 and 2018 to assess this query.

Mapping Tiers of Grocery Store Access in NYC

New York City has a substantial amount of residents residing in food apartheid. There are numerous and complex reasons as to why food apartheid exists, one of them being exclusionary zoning. This project assesses distribution of full-service grocery stores relative to the distribution of corner stores, grocers, and big-box food retail stores. Boroughs in NYC with the least access to full-service grocery stores are further ascertained. The analysis features percentage of low-income households, percentage of Hispanic or Latino origin by race populations, as well as the percentage of white populations throughout the 5 boroughs. Through the spatial analysis, it was found that low-income boroughs with higher percentages of Hispanic or Latino origin by race populations have more access to corner stores and less access to grocers, full-service grocery stores, and big box stores. This can be seen as a symptom of food apartheid.

Migration and Climate Change in Senegal

Climate change - resulting in food insecurity and financial instability - are forcing people to find better opportunities away from home. This project analizes a recent time period (2015-2019) relative to a baseline for NDVI vegetative values within Senegal. As agricultural productivity and NDVI values are generally related, this study finds negative anomalies to be a risk factor that can point to drought and stress conditions which can contribute to both food insecurity and financial instability.

Rent Burden and Potential Solutions in New York City

This spatial analysis considers locations and neighborhoods in New York City that are particularly rent burdened (spending more than 30% of household income on monthly rent) or severely rent-burdened (spending more than 50% of household income on monthly rent). This project also maps the vacant properties and hotels listed in New York City that could be possible points of conversion for affordable housing. Resulting cartography reveals that most of the same neighborhoods with rent-burdened and severely rent-burdened households are also in neighborhoods with a higher density of vacancies and a greater number of hotel locations. These neighborhoods were most prominently Midtown Manhattan and Williamsburg Brooklyn.

The River Nile Flood of September 2020 Khartoum, Sudan

The Nile River is considered to be the longest river in the world (approximately 4,258 miles). It flows through 11 countries starting from east Africa north into the Mediterranean Sea. Due to climate change, an increase in floods in the River Niles surrounding areas results in farm lands and homes vulnerable to its wrath. The Blue and White Nile meet in the city of Khartoum, Sudan. Most recently, Sudan was struck with many unprecedented challenges; an overthrow in a thirty year regime rule, an entire reconstruction of a transitional government, economic hardship, and most recently one of the worst floods the country has ever experienced. Khartoum was affected the most out of other regions, a staggering 103 thousand people were displaced according to a report by UN- OCHA Sudan. This project assesses impacted land area where the Blue and White Nile meet in Khartoum as a result of the recent flooding events, September 2020.
